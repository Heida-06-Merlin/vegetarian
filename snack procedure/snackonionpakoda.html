Onion Pakoda

Ingredients

  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup chickpea flour (besan)
  • 2 tablespoons rice flour
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon carom seeds (ajwain)
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying
  • 2-3 tablespoons chopped fresh coriander leaves
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped (optional)
  • Water, as needed

Procedure

  • In a mixing bowl, combine chickpea flour, rice flour, cumin seeds, carom seeds, red chili powder, turmeric powder, baking soda, and salt.
  • Add thinly sliced onions, chopped coriander leaves, and green chilies (if using) to the dry mixture. Mix well.
  • Sprinkle a little water at a time and mix until the onions are well coated with the flour mixture. The mixture should be thick enough to hold its shape when dropped.
  • Heat oil for deep frying in a pan or kadhai over medium heat.
  • Drop small portions of the onion mixture into the hot oil using your fingers or a spoon. Fry in batches, making sure not to overcrowd the pan.
  • Fry until the pakoras are golden brown and crispy, stirring occasionally for even frying. Remove from the oil using a slotted spoon and drain excess oil on paper towels.
  • Repeat the frying process with the remaining onion mixture.
  • Serve hot onion pakoras with mint chutney or tomato ketchup. Enjoy!
