Brinjal Rice

Ingredients

  • 1 cup basmati rice, washed and soaked for 30 minutes
  • 1 medium-sized brinjal (eggplant), diced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Procedure

  • Heat oil in a pan over medium heat.
  • Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  • Add cumin seeds and asafoetida. Sauté for a few seconds.
  • Add chopped onions and green chilies. Sauté until onions turn translucent.
  • Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell disappears.
  • Add diced brinjal and cook until it turns soft and tender.
  • Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become mushy.
  • Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well.
  • Add soaked and drained basmati rice. Stir gently to coat the rice with the masala.
  • Add 2 cups of water and bring it to a boil.
  • Reduce the heat to low, cover the pan, and let it simmer for about 15-20 minutes or until the rice is cooked and water is absorbed.
  • Once done, fluff the rice gently with a fork.
  • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  • Serve hot with raita or yogurt.
