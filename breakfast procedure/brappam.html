Appam

Ingredients

  • 1 cup raw rice
  • 1/4 cup grated coconut (fresh or frozen)
  • 1/4 cup cooked rice
  • 1/2 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/4 cup lukewarm water
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Procedure

  • Soak the raw rice in water for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  • In a small bowl, dissolve yeast and sugar in lukewarm water. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until frothy.
  • Grind soaked rice, grated coconut, and cooked rice together into a smooth batter using a blender or grinder.
  • Add the yeast mixture and salt to the batter. Mix well.
  • Cover the batter and let it ferment in a warm place for 6-8 hours or overnight.
  • Heat an appam pan over medium heat and lightly grease it with oil.
  • Pour a ladleful of batter into the center of the pan and quickly swirl the pan to spread the batter evenly in a thin layer towards the edges.
  • Cover the pan and cook for 2-3 minutes until the edges turn golden brown and crisp, and the center is cooked through.
  • Remove the appam from the pan and repeat with the remaining batter.
  • Serve hot with coconut milk, stew, or any desired accompaniment.
