Rava Upma

Ingredients

  • 1 cup semolina (rava)
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 green chili, chopped
  • 1-inch piece ginger, grated
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1/4 cup chopped vegetables (carrots, peas, beans, etc.)
  • A few curry leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • Coriander leaves for garnish (optional)

Procedure

  • Heat oil or ghee in a pan over medium heat.
  • Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
  • Add chopped onions, green chili, grated ginger, slit green chilies, and curry leaves. Sauté until onions turn translucent.
  • Add chopped vegetables and sauté for a few minutes until they are slightly cooked.
  • Add semolina (rava) and roast it on medium-low heat until it turns aromatic and light golden brown. Stir continuously to avoid burning.
  • Boil water separately in another pot.
  • Slowly pour the boiling water into the roasted semolina while stirring continuously to prevent lumps from forming.
  • Add salt to taste and mix well.
  • Cover and cook on low heat for 3-4 minutes or until the rava is cooked and water is absorbed.
  • Once cooked, fluff up the upma with a fork.
  • Garnish with chopped coriander leaves (optional).
  • Serve hot as is or with chutney or pickle.
