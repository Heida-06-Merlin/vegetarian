Adai

Ingredients

  • 1 cup raw rice
  • 1/4 cup parboiled rice (idli rice)
  • 1/4 cup split pigeon peas (toor dal)
  • 1/4 cup split black gram (urad dal)
  • 2 tablespoons split chickpeas (chana dal)
  • 2-3 dried red chilies
  • 1 inch ginger, chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 1/4 cup grated coconut (optional)
  • Salt to taste

Procedure

  • Wash and soak raw rice, parboiled rice, toor dal, urad dal, and chana dal together in water for 4-6 hours.
  • Drain the water and grind the soaked mixture into a coarse batter along with dried red chilies, chopped ginger, green chilies, and curry leaves. Add little water if needed to aid grinding.
  • Transfer the batter to a bowl, add grated coconut (if using) and salt. Mix well.
  • Heat a non-stick pan or griddle over medium heat.
  • Take a ladleful of batter and spread it on the pan in a circular motion to form a thick pancake (adai). You can make it as thin or thick as you prefer.
  • Drizzle oil around the edges of the adai and cook until the bottom turns golden brown and crisp.
  • Flip the adai and cook the other side until golden brown and cooked through.
  • Repeat with the remaining batter to make more adai.
  • Serve hot with avial, coconut chutney, or jaggery.
