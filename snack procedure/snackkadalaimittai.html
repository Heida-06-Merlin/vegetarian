Kadalai Mittai

Ingredients

  • 1 cup peanuts (raw or roasted)
  • 1 cup jaggery (grated or chopped)
  • 1 tablespoon ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder

Procedure

  • Heat a heavy-bottomed pan or kadai over medium heat.
  • Add the peanuts to the pan and dry roast them until they turn golden brown and fragrant. Keep stirring to avoid burning.
  • Once roasted, remove the peanuts from the pan and allow them to cool slightly.
  • Remove the skins from the peanuts by rubbing them between your palms or using a clean kitchen towel.
  • Grease a plate or tray with ghee and keep it ready.
  • In the same pan, add the grated or chopped jaggery along with a tablespoon of ghee.
  • Allow the jaggery to melt and cook until it reaches the soft-ball stage. To check if the jaggery syrup is ready, drop a small amount of syrup into a bowl of water. It should form a soft ball when pressed between your fingers.
  • Once the jaggery syrup reaches the desired consistency, add the roasted peanuts and cardamom powder to it. Mix well to coat the peanuts evenly with the syrup.
  • Quickly transfer the mixture to the greased plate or tray and spread it out evenly using a spatula or greased hands.
  • Allow the mixture to cool slightly and then cut it into desired shapes using a knife or cookie cutter while it's still warm.
  • Once completely cooled, store the Kadalai Mittai in an airtight container.
  • Enjoy this delicious and nutritious Peanut Chikki as a snack!
