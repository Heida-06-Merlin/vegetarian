Vaalathandu Kootu

Ingredients

  • 1 cup chopped vaalathandu (banana stem)
  • 1/2 cup moong dal (split green gram)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt, or to taste
  • For Grinding:
    • 1/2 cup grated coconut
    • 2 green chilies
    • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • For Tempering:
    • 1 tablespoon coconut oil
    • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
    • 1 teaspoon urad dal (split black gram)
    • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
    • A few curry leaves

Procedure

  • Wash and chop the vaalathandu (banana stem) into small pieces. Place them in a bowl of water with a little lemon juice to prevent discoloration.
  • Cook moong dal in a pressure cooker with turmeric powder and enough water until soft and mushy.
  • In a blender, grind grated coconut, green chilies, and cumin seeds into a smooth paste. Set aside.
  • Drain the chopped vaalathandu and add it to a pot with water. Cook until tender.
  • Once the vaalathandu is cooked, add the cooked moong dal and ground coconut paste. Mix well and let it simmer for a few minutes.
  • Adjust salt according to taste.
  • For tempering, heat coconut oil in a small pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Then add urad dal, asafoetida, and curry leaves. Fry until dal turns golden brown.
  • Pour the tempering over the kootu and mix well.
  • Serve hot with steamed rice or chapati.
