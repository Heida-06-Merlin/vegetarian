Appalam Kuzhambu

Ingredients

  • 5-6 appalams (papadums)
  • 1 cup tamarind extract
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2-3 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • 1 teaspoon sambar powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon asafoetida (hing)
  • A few curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Coriander leaves for garnish

Procedure

  • Heat sesame oil in a pan. Fry the appalams until they become crispy. Set aside.
  • Heat sesame oil in a separate pan. Add mustard seeds and fenugreek seeds. Let them splutter.
  • Add chopped onions, crushed garlic, and curry leaves. Saut√© until onions turn golden brown.
  • Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy.
  • Add sambar powder, turmeric powder, and asafoetida. Mix well.
  • Add tamarind extract, salt, and water (if needed). Bring it to a boil.
  • Simmer for a few minutes until the kuzhambu thickens.
  • Break the fried appalams into small pieces and add them to the kuzhambu.
  • Allow it to simmer for a couple of minutes to let the flavors meld.
  • Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and serve hot with rice.
