Pakoda

Ingredients

  • 1 cup chickpea flour (besan)
  • 1 large potato, thinly sliced
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 green chili, finely chopped (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon carom seeds (ajwain)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon chaat masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • Water as needed
  • Oil for frying

Procedure

  • In a large mixing bowl, combine chickpea flour, carom seeds, cumin seeds, turmeric powder, red chili powder, chaat masala, and salt.
  • Add sliced potatoes, onions, green chili (if using), and fresh coriander leaves to the flour mixture. Mix well to coat the vegetables evenly.
  • Sprinkle a little water at a time and mix until you get a thick batter-like consistency that coats the vegetables well.
  • Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat.
  • Take small portions of the batter and carefully drop them into the hot oil using a spoon or your hands.
  • Fry the pakoras in batches until they turn golden brown and crispy, turning them occasionally for even cooking.
  • Remove the pakoras from the oil using a slotted spoon and drain excess oil on paper towels.
  • Serve hot with mint chutney or ketchup.
