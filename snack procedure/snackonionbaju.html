Onion Bhaji

Ingredients

  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup chickpea flour (besan)
  • 2 tablespoons rice flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Water, as needed
  • Oil for deep frying

Procedure

  • In a large mixing bowl, combine chickpea flour, rice flour, baking powder, cumin seeds, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt.
  • Add sliced onions to the flour mixture and mix well, ensuring all onions are evenly coated.
  • Sprinkle water little by little and mix until the onions are well coated and the mixture forms a thick batter. The batter should be thick enough to coat the back of a spoon.
  • Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat.
  • Drop spoonfuls of the onion mixture into the hot oil and fry in batches until golden brown and crispy, stirring occasionally to ensure even cooking.
  • Once cooked, remove the onion bhajis from the oil using a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  • Serve hot with mint chutney or tamarind chutney.
