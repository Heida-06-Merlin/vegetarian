Potato Thenguzhal

Ingredients

  • 2 cups rice flour
  • 2 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1/2 cup coconut, grated
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon asafoetida
  • Salt to taste
  • Water, as needed
  • Oil for deep frying

Procedure

  • In a large mixing bowl, combine rice flour, mashed potatoes, grated coconut, chopped green chilies, cumin seeds, asafoetida, and salt.
  • Add water gradually and knead into a smooth dough. The dough should be soft and pliable.
  • Heat oil in a deep frying pan or kadhai over medium heat.
  • Meanwhile, fill a murukku press or chakli maker with a portion of the dough.
  • Press the dough directly into the hot oil in a circular motion to form spiral-shaped thenguzhal. You can also make small rounds or shapes as desired.
  • Fry the thenguzhal until they turn golden brown and crispy. Make sure to fry in batches and do not overcrowd the pan.
  • Remove the fried thenguzhal using a slotted spoon and drain excess oil on paper towels.
  • Repeat the frying process with the remaining dough.
  • Once cooled, store the crispy Potato Thenguzhal in an airtight container. They can be enjoyed as a snack or with tea.
