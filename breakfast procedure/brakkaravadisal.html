Akkaravadisal

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup raw rice
  • 1/4 cup moong dal (split yellow lentils)
  • 1/4 cup chana dal (split chickpeas)
  • 1/4 cup jaggery, grated
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup ghee (clarified butter)
  • 10-12 cashew nuts
  • 10-12 raisins
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • A pinch of saffron strands (optional)
  • 2 cups milk
  • Water as needed

Procedure

  • Rinse raw rice, moong dal, and chana dal together. Drain well and set aside.
  • In a pressure cooker, add the rinsed rice and dal mixture along with 2 cups of milk and water as needed. Pressure cook for 3-4 whistles or until the rice and dal are cooked well.
  • Once the pressure releases naturally, open the cooker and mash the rice and dal mixture well.
  • Add grated jaggery and sugar to the mashed rice-dal mixture. Mix well until the jaggery and sugar dissolve completely.
  • In a separate pan, heat ghee over medium heat. Fry cashew nuts and raisins until golden brown. Remove from heat and set aside.
  • Add the fried cashew nuts, raisins, cardamom powder, and saffron strands (if using) to the rice-dal mixture. Mix well.
  • Cook the mixture on low heat for 5-10 minutes, stirring continuously, until the akkaravadisal thickens and reaches a pudding-like consistency.
  • Remove from heat and serve hot or cold as per preference.
