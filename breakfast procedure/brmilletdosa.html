Millet Dosa

Ingredients

  • 1 cup millet flour (any variety such as finger millet (ragi), pearl millet (bajra), or foxtail millet)
  • 1/4 cup rice flour
  • 1/4 cup urad dal (black gram lentils), soaked for 4-6 hours
  • 1/2 inch piece ginger, grated
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • A handful of coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • Oil for cooking

Procedure

  • Drain the soaked urad dal and grind it into a smooth batter. Add water as needed to achieve a thick and smooth consistency.
  • In a mixing bowl, combine millet flour, rice flour, grated ginger, chopped green chilies, chopped coriander leaves, cumin seeds, and salt.
  • Add the ground urad dal batter to the dry ingredients and mix well to form a smooth batter. Add water gradually to achieve a dosa batter consistency.
  • Cover the batter and let it ferment overnight or for 6-8 hours in a warm place.
  • Heat a dosa tawa or griddle over medium heat.
  • Stir the batter well and pour it onto the hot tawa in a circular motion from the outer edges to the center to form a thin dosa.
  • Drizzle some oil around the edges of the dosa.
  • Cook the dosa until it turns golden brown and crisp on the bottom.
  • Flip the dosa and cook the other side until it is cooked through.
  • Repeat the process with the remaining batter.
  • Serve hot with chutney, sambar, or any side dish of your choice.
