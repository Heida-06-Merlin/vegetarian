Samosa

Ingredients

  • For the dough:
    • 2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1/4 cup vegetable oil or ghee
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt
    • Cold water, as needed
  • For the filling:
    • 3 medium potatoes, boiled, peeled, and mashed
    • 1 cup green peas, boiled or thawed if frozen
    • 2 tablespoons oil
    • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    • 1 onion, finely chopped
    • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
    • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
    • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1 teaspoon garam masala
    • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
    • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
    • Salt to taste
    • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped

Procedure

  • Prepare the dough by mixing all-purpose flour, salt, and oil/ghee until it resembles breadcrumbs.
  • Add cold water gradually and knead into a firm dough. Cover and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  • For the filling, heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  • Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  • Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for a minute.
  • Add mashed potatoes, boiled peas, and all the spices. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  • Remove from heat and let the filling cool down.
  • Divide the dough into equal-sized balls and roll each ball into a thin circle.
  • Cut each circle in half to form semi-circles.
  • Take one semi-circle and fold it into a cone shape, sealing the edges with water.
  • Fill the cone with the prepared filling and seal the top edge tightly.
  • Repeat with the remaining dough and filling.
  • Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat.
  • Gently slide the prepared samosas into the hot oil and fry until golden brown and crispy.
  • Remove from the oil and drain on paper towels.
  • Serve hot with mint chutney or tamarind chutney.
