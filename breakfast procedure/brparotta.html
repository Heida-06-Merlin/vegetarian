Parotta

Ingredients

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • Water as needed
  • Oil or ghee for frying

Procedure

  • In a large mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, sugar, salt, and ghee or oil.
  • Add water gradually and knead the mixture into a soft, smooth, and non-sticky dough.
  • Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for about 2 hours.
  • Divide the dough into small lemon-sized balls.
  • Take a dough ball and flatten it with your palms into a thin disc.
  • Brush some oil or ghee on the flattened dough disc.
  • Make cuts on the flattened disc with a knife or pizza cutter without cutting through completely.
  • Roll up the flattened disc tightly into a coil, similar to rolling a carpet.
  • Tuck the end of the coil underneath the rolled dough to secure it.
  • Press down the rolled dough gently with your palms to flatten it slightly.
  • Repeat the process with the remaining dough balls.
  • Heat oil or ghee in a frying pan over medium heat.
  • Place the rolled dough on the hot pan and cook until golden brown and crispy on both sides.
  • Remove the parotta from the pan and drain excess oil on paper towels.
  • Repeat the process with the remaining dough balls.
  • Serve hot with curry or any side dish of your choice.
