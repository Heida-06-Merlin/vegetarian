Mulangi Sambar (Radish Sambar)

Ingredients

  • 1 cup toor dal (split pigeon peas)
  • 1 small radish, peeled and chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 lemon-sized tamarind ball
  • 2 tablespoons sambar powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • A few curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Coriander leaves for garnish

Procedure

  • Cook toor dal in a pressure cooker until soft. Mash and set aside.
  • Soak tamarind in warm water and extract the juice.
  • Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds, fenugreek seeds, and curry leaves. Let them splutter.
  • Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent.
  • Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft.
  • Add chopped radish and sauté for a few minutes.
  • Add tamarind extract, sambar powder, turmeric powder, asafoetida, and salt to taste. Mix well.
  • Add cooked toor dal and enough water to adjust the consistency. Mix well.
  • Bring the sambar to a boil and simmer for 10-15 minutes until the radish is cooked through.
  • Adjust salt if needed. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
  • Mulangi Sambar is ready to be served. Serve hot with rice.
