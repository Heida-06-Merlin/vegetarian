Poori

Ingredients

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour (atta)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon ghee or oil
  • Water as needed
  • Oil for deep frying

Procedure

  • In a large mixing bowl, combine whole wheat flour, salt, and ghee or oil.
  • Add water gradually and knead the mixture into a smooth and stiff dough. The dough should not be too soft or too firm.
  • Divide the dough into small lemon-sized balls.
  • Roll each ball into a smooth ball between your palms and flatten it slightly.
  • Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat.
  • Roll out each ball into a small circle using a rolling pin on a lightly floured surface. The poori should be about 4-5 inches in diameter.
  • Once the oil is hot, carefully slide the rolled poori into the hot oil.
  • Press gently with a slotted spoon until it puffs up. Then, flip and cook until golden brown on both sides.
  • Remove the poori from the oil and drain excess oil on paper towels.
  • Repeat the process with the remaining dough balls.
  • Serve hot with potato masala, chana masala, or any curry of your choice.
